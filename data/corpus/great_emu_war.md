# The Great Emu War

The Great Emu War was a wildlife management operation carried out in Western
Australia in late 1932. After the First World War, many veterans had taken up
wheat farming in the Campion district. By 1932 around 20,000 emus migrated into
the farmland during their breeding season, trampling crops and damaging fences.

The farmers appealed for help, and the Minister of Defence, George Pearce,
deployed soldiers armed with two Lewis machine guns. The operation was led by
Major G. P. W. Meredith. The emus proved remarkably hard to hit: they scattered
into small groups and ran at speed, and on one occasion a machine gun jammed
after felling only about a dozen birds.

After roughly a week and thousands of rounds expended for only a few hundred
emus killed, the military withdrew. The press treated the episode as a comedy,
and the emus were widely declared the victors. Farmers later returned to
bounty systems, which culled far more birds than the army had.
